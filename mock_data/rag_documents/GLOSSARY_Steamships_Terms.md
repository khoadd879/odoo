# Glossary — Steamships Terms and Acronyms

**Document ID:** GLOSSARY-2026
**Version:** 3.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Owner:** Sales Operations
**Visibility:** CLIENT-FACING (used by client mode of AI chatbot)

---

## A

**AI Chatbot** — The Steamships AI assistant in Odoo (menu: Ask AI). Answers staff and client questions based on the company's own documents. Always cites the source document.

**AP** — Accounts Payable. Money the company owes to suppliers.

**AR** — Accounts Receivable. Money clients owe to the company.

## B

**BAR** — Best Available Rate. The standard (non-discounted) hotel rate for walk-in and last-minute bookings.

**Bill of Lading (B/L)** — The official shipping paper that travels with cargo. Records the shipper, consignee, vessel, container numbers, ports, and cargo description. Used as a contract of carriage and a receipt for goods.

## C

**CAM** — Common Area Maintenance. The service charge in a commercial lease covering shared services (security, cleaning, landscaping, building management).

**Consort** — The Steamships shipping division that operates the container fleet.

**CRM** — Customer Relationship Management. The Odoo system that tracks every client interaction, document, and opportunity.

**Cut-off** — The deadline by which cargo documentation or cargo itself must be at the port. After the cut-off, the cargo misses the vessel.

## D

**DG** — Dangerous Goods. Cargo classified under the IMO IMDG Code (explosives, gases, flammable liquids, oxidizers, toxic, radioactive, corrosives, miscellaneous).

**DGD** — Dangerous Goods Declaration. The mandatory document for DG cargo (see SOP-SHIP-007).

## E

**ETD / ETA** — Estimated Time of Departure / Arrival. The scheduled (not guaranteed) time a vessel leaves or arrives at a port.

**Export Permit** — A government authorisation required to export regulated PNG goods (logs, fish, minerals, coffee).

## F

**FCL** — Full Container Load. A booking where the client fills an entire container (20ft, 40ft, or 40ft HC).

## G

**GHC** — Gross Harvested Cubic. The volume measurement used for LCL pricing (per cubic metre).

## I

**IMDG** — International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code. The IMO standard for classifying, packaging, marking, and documenting dangerous goods on ships.

**INCOTERMS** — International Commercial Terms (2020 edition). Defines buyer and seller responsibilities for shipping, insurance, and customs clearance. Common terms: FOB, CIF, EXW, DDP.

**IPA** — Investment Promotion Authority. PNG authority that issues Certificates of Incorporation.

**IRC** — Internal Revenue Commission. PNG tax authority that issues Tax Identification Numbers (TIN).

## K

**KYC** — Know Your Customer. The 8-item checklist every new client must complete before they can transact (see SOP-CRM-001).

## L

**LCL** — Less than Container Load. A booking where the client's cargo shares a container with other clients' cargo. Priced per cubic metre.

## N

**NDC** — Non-Delivery Certificate. Document issued when cargo cannot be delivered to the consignee.

## O

**Odoo** — The enterprise system used by Steamships. Runs CRM, sales, inventory, accounting, HR, and the AI chatbot.

## P

**PGK** — Papua New Guinea Kina. Local currency.

**POM** — Port Moresby (the capital city, also a major port).

**Port of Loading (POL)** — The port where cargo is loaded onto the vessel.

**Port of Discharge (POD)** — The port where cargo is unloaded from the vessel.

## R

**RAG** — Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The technique that powers the Steamships AI Chatbot — it searches the company documents first, then asks the AI to write an answer using only those documents.

**Reefer** — A refrigerated container (or vessel slot) for perishable cargo.

## S

**SOP** — Standard Operating Procedure. The official "how to do this task" instruction. Examples: SOP-SHIP-001 (container booking), SOP-CRM-001 (client onboarding).

**Stevedoring** — The loading and unloading of vessels at a port.

## T

**TIN** — Tax Identification Number. Issued by the IRC. Required for every client and every employee.

**TEU** — Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit. The standard measure of container capacity. One 20ft container = 1 TEU. One 40ft container = 2 TEU.

## U

**UN Number** — The four-digit number assigned by the UN to identify dangerous goods (e.g. UN1230 = methanol).
