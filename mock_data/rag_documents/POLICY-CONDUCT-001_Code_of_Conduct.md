# POLICY-CONDUCT-001 — Code of Conduct

**Version:** 4.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01

**Approved by:** Board of Directors
**Visibility:** STAFF ONLY (also published to new hires in the employment contract)

---

## 1. Purpose

Set out the ethical standards expected of every employee, contractor, and director of Steamships Trading Company. The Code is not optional; breach is grounds for disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

## 2. Core principles

We expect every person acting for Steamships to:

1. Act with integrity in every business interaction.
2. Comply with the law of every country where we operate.
3. Treat colleagues, clients, suppliers, and communities with respect.
4. Avoid conflicts of interest; declare any potential conflict promptly.
5. Protect company assets and client information.
6. Speak up when something does not look right (see Section 9 — Whistleblowing).

## 3. Anti-bribery and anti-corruption

Steamships has a ZERO TOLERANCE policy on bribery. Specifically:

- No employee may offer, give, solicit, or accept any payment, gift, or favour intended to influence a business decision.
- Gifts from suppliers or clients valued at more than PGK 200 must be declared in the Gifts Register (Odoo menu: Compliance → Gifts Register).
- Hospitality (meals, event tickets) is acceptable if reasonable, infrequent, and in the ordinary course of business.
- Engaging with PNG government officials requires Legal sign-off and is logged in the Government Interactions Register.

## 4. Conflicts of interest

All employees must declare:

- Any direct or indirect financial interest in a supplier, client, or competitor.
- Any close family member working for a supplier, client, or competitor.
- Any outside employment or directorships.

Declarations are made annually (in Odoo) and updated whenever a new conflict arises. Failure to declare is treated as a serious breach.

## 5. Confidentiality

Information acquired in the course of employment is confidential and remains so after employment ends. Specific rules:

- Client data is Level 3 (CONFIDENTIAL) — see POLICY-DATA-001.
- Pricing details, contracts, and forecasts are confidential.
- The contents of the Steamships AI Chatbot queries and responses are logged for audit purposes.

## 6. Use of company resources

Company resources (laptops, phones, vehicles, internet) are for business use. Limited personal use is acceptable; excessive personal use is not. Company resources must not be used for:

- Personal commercial activity.
- Illegal activity (including pirating software or media).
- Harassment of any kind.
- Political campaigning.

## 7. Alcohol, drugs, and weapons

- Staff must not be under the influence of alcohol or non-prescribed drugs while on duty or on company premises.
- Weapons are prohibited on company premises except for authorised security personnel.

## 8. Dress code

Business attire is required in client-facing roles and at meetings. Site visits require PPE (long sleeves, long pants, safety boots, hard hat, hi-vis vest) — provided by the Company.

## 9. Whistleblowing

Steamships encourages reporting of any suspected breach of the Code, illegal activity, or risk to the company. Reports may be made:

- To your Line Manager.
- To HR (hr.complaints@steamships.com.pg).
- Via the independent Whistleblower Hotline: 1800 1234 (toll-free, anonymous option available).

Whistleblowers are protected from retaliation. Any retaliation is itself a serious breach of the Code.

## 10. Breaches and discipline

Breaches are investigated by HR and/or Internal Audit. Outcomes may include:

- Informal coaching.
- Written warning.
- Final warning.
- Dismissal.
- Referral to law enforcement (for criminal conduct).
